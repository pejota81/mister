# Mister v0.10 — Match Day Experience & Fixture Balancing

> Released: 2026-05-02

---

## Richer Match Day Screen

The Match Day results screen has been completely redesigned with five information panels.

### Attendance

Each match now reports the crowd size based on your club's stadium capacity and a home-attendance percentage derived from form and league position.

### Strength Comparison Bar

A visual split bar shows the relative pre-match strength of both teams (home vs away) so you can immediately see how evenly the sides were matched.

### Player Ratings

Every player who featured in the match receives a rating from 1 to 10, colour-coded for quick scanning:

| Colour | Range | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| 🟢 Green | 7 – 10 | Solid or outstanding |
| 🟡 Amber | 5 – 6  | Adequate |
| 🔴 Red   | 1 – 4  | Poor     |

Players who entered as substitutes are labelled accordingly.

### Cup Results Panel

When cup matches were played in the same week, a dedicated panel lists every cup result and highlights your club's tie if you were involved.

### Momentum Bar

A 0–100 bar shows your club's current momentum with a contextual label (e.g. *On a roll*, *Building*, *Struggling*). Winning streaks push momentum up; defeats pull it down.

### Back to Dashboard

The primary button on the Match Day screen now returns you directly to the **Dashboard** instead of cycling back to Tactics, keeping the post-match flow clean.

---

## Half-Time Substitutions

The match is now split into two halves. After the first half resolves you land on a dedicated **Half-Time screen** before the second half plays out.

### What you can do at half-time

- See the **first-half score** and each player's **first-half rating**
- Make up to **3 substitutions** — click a starting player to sub them off, then click a bench player to bring them on
- Click **Continue** to simulate the second half with the updated lineup

### Player ratings at full time

- Players who played both halves receive an **average** of their two half-ratings
- Substitutes who only played one half keep their single-half rating
- Players who were substituted off but did not come back have no second-half rating

### Half-time on cup weeks (bye weeks)

If your league team has no fixture in a given week but you have a **cup match**, the half-time screen now appears for cup ties too — including two-legged rounds. Extra time and penalties are still resolved automatically after the second half.

## Fixture Scheduling — No Back-to-Back Same-Venue Games

Regular-season fixtures are now post-processed after generation to eliminate consecutive same-venue runs.

### The problem

The circle method used to build the round-robin schedule always places the "fixed" team at home in every first-leg round. This produced patterns like **H H H H A A A A** for some clubs.

### The fix

A `balanceHomeAway()` pass runs after the raw schedule is produced:

1. For every club, find consecutive H-H or A-A pairs in the calendar
2. For the conflicting fixture, locate the **other leg** of the same matchup (the reverse fixture)
3. **Swap only the calendar-week assignments** between the two legs — the home/away designation never changes, so the double round-robin structure remains valid
4. Keep the swap only if it reduces the total conflict count; otherwise revert
5. Repeat (up to 30 passes) until no further improvement is possible

Cup fixtures are intentionally left unaffected — venue assignment in cup ties remains random.

## Breaking Change — Saved Games

The `AppState` schema has new fields. Old saves will continue to load but will start with default values for the new state.

| Field | Default |
|-------|---------|
| `halfTime` | `null` |
| `lastCupMatches` | `[]` |

No data loss; existing saves do not need a new game.
