# Mister v0.07 — Division-Aware Players & Match Realism

> Released: 2026-05-02

---

## Division-Stratified Player Skills

Player skills now accurately reflect the league they play in. Higher-ranked divisions produce better players on average, while lower divisions are weaker — but with enough overlap that a standout talent from a smaller league can match a mid-table player from a bigger one.

### Skill Bands by Division Rank

Each league (ranked 1–9 globally by IFFHS prestige) has its own skill range. Players are generated within that band, plus a club-level bias based on historical standing:

| Rank | Example League | Skill Range |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | Premier League | 78 – 99 |
| 2 | Brazil Série A | 74 – 95 |
| 3 | Serie A | 70 – 91 |
| 4 | La Liga | 66 – 87 |
| 5 | Bundesliga | 62 – 83 |
| 6 | Ligue 1 | 58 – 79 |
| 7 | Primeira Liga | 54 – 75 |
| 8 | Eredivisie | 50 – 71 |
| 9 | Championship | 46 – 67 |

- Top-division players can now reach **skill 99**
- Youth players generated during season aging use the same bands, so academies in lower leagues produce weaker prospects

---

## Club Historical Rankings

Each club's starting squad quality and finances now reflect its historical standing within its league. All 176 clubs across 9 leagues have been ranked by their historical track record.

### How It Works

- **Rank 1 club** in a league generates players from the **top of its skill range** (±12.5% bias)
- **Last-ranked club** generates players from the **bottom of its range**
- This creates realistic in-league variation: Real Madrid and Girona both play La Liga, but their squads reflect their very different histories

### Starting Balance by Division and Rank

Starting balance scales linearly from minimum (last-placed club) to maximum (top club):

| Division Tier | Min Balance | Max Balance |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Top flight | $5M | $30M |
| Second tier | $2M | $12M |

## Division-Aware Salaries

Player salaries now reflect the financial reality of their league. The same skill level earns very different wages depending on division prestige.

### Salary Multiplier

Multipliers scale linearly from rank 1 (richest) to rank 9 (poorest):

| Rank | Multiplier | Skill-73 weekly wage |
|------|------------|---------------------|
| 1 (Premier League) | ×4.00 | $24,000 |
| 2 | ×3.58 | $21,480 |
| 3 | ×3.15 | $18,900 |
| 4 | ×2.73 | $16,380 |
| 5 | ×2.30 | $13,800 |
| 6 | ×1.88 | $11,280 |
| 7 | ×1.45 | $8,700 |
| 8 | ×1.03 | $6,180 |
| 9 (Championship) | ×0.60 | $3,600 |

- Salaries are recalculated every season end as part of aging
- Contract renewals, youth wages, and market values all use the same formula
- **Transfer market implication:** moving a player from a lower league to the Premier League means a significant wage increase at renewal time

## Per-Player Match Performance

Each player now has an individual performance factor every match, creating realistic variance — even world-class players can have an off day.

### How It Works

At the start of each match simulation, every starting XI player is assigned a **performance factor** (0.75–1.25) which multiplies their effective skill for that game:

```
effectiveSkill = skill × fitnessFactor × performanceFactor
```

### Stamina Controls Consistency

A player's **stamina** attribute determines how wide their performance variance is:

| Stamina | Variance | Possible factor |
|---------|----------|-----------------|
| 99 | ±0% | ~1.00 (rock-solid) |
| 80 | ±3% | 0.97–1.03 |
| 60 | ±6% | 0.94–1.06 |
| 40 | ±18% | 0.82–1.18 (boom-or-bust) |

- Results remain **deterministic** — the same fixture seed always produces the same ratings
- A single bad performance from a key player can swing a match result

### Player Ratings

Every match result now includes a **per-player rating (1–10)** for all 22 starting players:

- Rating 1 = terrible day (factor 0.75)
- Rating 6 = average (factor 1.0)
- Rating 10 = exceptional (factor 1.25)

Ratings are stored in `MatchResult.playerRatings` and available for display throughout the UI.

---

## Breaking Change — Saved Games

- Existing saves load correctly; `playerRatings` will be absent on past match results (displayed as unavailable)
- `division.iffhsRank` is a new field — older saves missing it will use neutral salary multipliers until the next data refresh
- **Recommendation:** re-run `node scripts/fetch-leagues.mjs` to regenerate `gamedata.json` with the new skill bands, club rankings, and salary data
